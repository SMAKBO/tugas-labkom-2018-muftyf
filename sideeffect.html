Side Effect of Cortisone

What side effects can be experienced because of cortisone?
All drugs can cause side effects, but many people do not experience, or know the side effects of the drug. Check with your doctor if the following common side effects do not disappear or worsen, such as:
• Difficulty sleeping
• Dizziness or light head feeling
• Headache
• Increased appetite
• Increased sweating
• Indigestion
• Restless

Immediately seek medical help if you experience any of the following severe side effects:
• Severe allergic reactions (rash, difficulty inkining, facial swelling, lips, tongue, or throat.)
• Black stool
• Changes in the menstrual cycle
• Chest pain
• Eye pain or increased pressure inside the eye
• Fever, chills or sore throat
• Bone or joint pain
• Seizures
• Severe or absent nausea and vomiting
• Abdominal pain and bloating
• Swelling of the feet
• Unusual increase or decrease in body weight
• Changes to vision
• Vomit that looks like a coffee collision

But not everyone has side effects above. There may be some side effects not mentioned above. If you have concerns about certain side effects, consult your doctor or pharmacist.
